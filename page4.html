4. Attendance Policy

4.1 Work Days and Holidays

4.1.1 Work Days

Standard Workdays: Employees are expected to work from Monday to Saturday each week.

Off Day: Sunday is designated as the regular off day for all employees.

4.1.2 Public Holidays

Observation of Public Holidays: All recognized public holidays are considered off days. Employees will not be required to work on these days unless approved by management.

Approval for Work on Public Holidays: In cases where business needs necessitate work on a public holiday, management may approve such requests on a case-by-case basis. Employees who work on a public holiday may be eligible for compensatory leave or additional incentives as determined by management.

4.2 Work Time and Attendance

4.2.1 Start Time (Work from Home)

Official Start Time: All employees must commence their workday by 10:00 AM.

4.2.2 Morning Meetings

Mandatory Attendance: Attendance at the daily morning meeting is mandatory for all employees.

Reporting Delays: If an employee is unable to start work by 10:00 AM, they must inform their immediate superior before the start time, providing a valid reason for the delay. Valid reasons may include medical emergencies, transportation issues, or other unforeseen circumstances. Failure to notify in advance may result in disciplinary action, including deductions in pay as outlined below.

4.2.3 Work Hours

Flexible Work Hours: Elite Resumes does not enforce a fixed number of working hours per day. Instead, employees are expected to complete their daily assigned tasks within the workday.

Task Completion Responsibility: Employees are responsible for managing their time effectively to ensure that all assigned tasks are completed by the end of the workday.

Consequences of Non-Completion: Failure to deliver assigned tasks may result in a deduction of half a day's pay. Continuous inability to meet task deadlines may lead to further disciplinary actions, including potential termination.

4.2.4 Monitoring and Availability

Communication Platforms: Employees must remain available on WhatsApp during designated work hours for communication, collaboration, and task updates.

Availability Requirements: Employees should not be unavailable for more than 30 minutes during work hours without prior notification. If an employee anticipates being unavailable, they must notify their higher official in advance, providing a valid reason.

Emergency Situations: In unforeseen emergencies, employees should inform their higher official as soon as possible.

Consequences of Unavailability: Being unavailable for more than 30 minutes without prior notification may result in a deduction of half a day's pay or a full day's pay loss, depending on the severity and frequency of the unavailability.

4.2.5 Power Outages, Connectivity Issues, and Technical Difficulties

Preparedness Requirement: All employees working remotely must ensure their devices, including phones and laptops, are fully charged before starting the workday to minimize disruption during potential power outages.

Reporting Technical Issues: In the event of a power outage, connectivity issue, or other technical difficulties that prevent an employee from performing their duties, they must immediately inform their immediate superior or designated contact via phone or any other available means.

Leave Application for Extended Disruptions: If the issue persists and the employee is unable to resume work for a significant portion of the work period, they must apply for leave for the time lost. Failure to do so may result in deductions from pay as outlined in the company's attendance policy.

Documentation of Issues: Employees may be required to provide documentation or details regarding the nature of the technical issue (e.g., power outage notices, screenshots of network issues) to support their leave request.

4.3 Payable Leaves

4.3.1 Casual Leave

Entitlement: Employees are entitled to one (1) casual leave with pay per month.

Usage: Casual leaves are intended for personal matters, emergencies, or unforeseen circumstances that require an employee's immediate attention. Employees must apply for casual leave in advance through the designated leave management system or inform their immediate supervisor as per company procedures.

4.3.2 Medical Leave

Entitlement: Employees are entitled to one (1) medical leave per month without the need to submit a medical certificate. For more than one (1) medical leave within a month, employees must provide a valid medical certificate issued by a recognized medical practitioner.

Payment for Additional Medical Leaves: Payment for medical leaves exceeding the monthly entitlement is subject to management's approval based on the circumstances and the validity of the medical certificate. Employees must submit all required documentation promptly to facilitate approval.

Usage: Medical leaves are intended for the recovery from illness, medical appointments, or any health-related issues requiring time off.

4.3.3 Leave Application Procedures

Advance Notice: Employees are encouraged to apply for leaves in advance whenever possible to ensure adequate staffing and workflow continuity.

Emergency Leaves: In cases of emergencies where advance notice is not feasible, employees must inform their immediate supervisor or higher official as soon as possible.

Approval Process: All leave requests must be submitted through the designated leave management system or as per company procedures. Supervisors and management will review leave requests based on operational requirements and provide timely approvals or rejections. Necessary documentation (e.g., medical certificates) must be provided when required.

4.3.4 Consequences of Misuse of Leave Policies

Unauthorized Leaves: Taking leaves without proper authorization or falsifying leave reasons may result in disciplinary action, including deductions in pay or termination of employment.

Exceeding Leave Entitlements: Repeatedly exceeding leave entitlements without valid reasons or management approval may lead to further disciplinary measures.
