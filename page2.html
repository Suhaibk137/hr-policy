2. Employment Practices

2.1 Recruitment and Selection

2.1.1 Job Posting Procedures

At Elite Resumes, we utilize multiple channels to attract a diverse pool of qualified candidates. Our job posting procedures include:

  • Social Media Platforms: Leveraging platforms such as LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram to advertise open positions and engage with potential candidates.
  • Job Portals: Posting vacancies on popular job portals like Indeed, Naukri, Glassdoor, and others relevant to our industry to reach a wide audience.
  • Employee Referrals: Encouraging current employees to refer qualified candidates through our referral program, fostering a collaborative recruitment environment.
  • Company Website: Listing available positions on our official website www.eliteresumes.co to attract candidates who are specifically interested in our services and company culture.

2.1.2 Hiring Criteria and Processes

Our hiring process is designed to identify and select the best-fit candidates who align with our company’s mission, vision, and values. The hiring criteria and processes include:

  • Application Review: HR personnel and hiring managers review applications to shortlist candidates based on qualifications, experience, and skills relevant to the position.
  • Initial Screening: Conducted via virtual platforms such as Google Meets and Zoom to assess candidates' suitability, communication skills, and cultural fit.
  • Interviews:
    • First Round: Typically conducted by HR representatives to evaluate general competencies and alignment with company values.
    • Second Round: Led by managers and team leaders to assess technical skills and role-specific expertise.
    • Final Round: May involve interviews with directors or executive leadership for higher-level positions to ensure strategic fit and leadership capabilities.
  • Assessment Tests: Depending on the role, candidates may be required to complete assessments or practical tests to demonstrate their proficiency and problem-solving abilities.
  • Reference Checks: Conducting reference checks to verify candidates’ professional backgrounds and performance history.
  • Decision Making: The hiring team collaborates to make informed decisions, ensuring fairness and consistency throughout the selection process.

2.1.3 Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) Commitments

Elite Resumes is committed to providing equal employment opportunities to all individuals, regardless of race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic as defined by applicable laws. Our EEO commitments include:

  • Non-Discrimination: Ensuring that all recruitment, hiring, promotion, and employment practices are free from discrimination and bias.
  • Inclusive Workplace: Promoting a diverse and inclusive work environment where all employees feel valued and respected.
  • Accessibility: Making reasonable accommodations for applicants and employees with disabilities to ensure they have equal access to opportunities and resources.
  • Training and Awareness: Providing regular training to HR personnel and hiring managers on EEO policies, anti-discrimination laws, and inclusive hiring practices to uphold our commitment to fairness and equality.

2.2 Onboarding

2.2.1 Orientation Programs

A structured orientation program is essential for integrating new employees into Elite Resumes effectively. Our orientation programs include:

  • Welcome Session: Introduction to the company’s mission, vision, values, and organizational structure.
  • Department Overviews: Presentations by different departments to help new hires understand their roles and how they contribute to the company’s objectives.
  • Meet and Greet: Opportunities to meet team members, managers, and key stakeholders to foster initial connections and teamwork.
  • Resource Provision: Providing access to company resources, tools, and systems necessary for their roles.

2.2.2 Documentation and Paperwork

To ensure a smooth onboarding process, the following documentation and paperwork are required:

  • Offer Letter: Issued to candidates upon acceptance of the job offer, outlining the terms and conditions of employment.
  • Employment Agreement: Detailed contract specifying job responsibilities, compensation, benefits, and other employment terms.
  • Personal Information Forms: Collection of necessary personal information for payroll, benefits enrollment, and compliance purposes.
  • Policy Acknowledgment: Employees are required to read and acknowledge the company’s HR policies, code of conduct, and confidentiality agreements.

2.2.3 Probationary Periods

All new employees undergo a probationary period to ensure mutual suitability and performance alignment. The probationary period details include:

  • Duration: A standard probationary period of one (1) month is applied to all new joiners, during which their performance and fit within the company are assessed.
  • Training: Training duration may vary based on the position’s requirements, ensuring that employees receive the necessary skills and knowledge to perform their roles effectively.
  • Performance Evaluation: Regular check-ins and evaluations are conducted to monitor progress, provide feedback, and address any challenges faced by the new employee.
  • Outcome:
    • Successful Completion: Upon successful completion of the probationary period, the employee receives a formal joining letter, confirming their permanent status within the company.
    • Extension or Termination: If an employee does not meet the required performance standards, the company may decide to:
      • Extend the Probation: Provide additional time for the employee to improve and meet the necessary criteria.
      • Termination: Proceed with termination of employment if sufficient improvement is not observed, following fair and legal procedures.
