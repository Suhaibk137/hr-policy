4. Attendance Policy

4.1 Work Days and Holidays

4.1.1 Work Days

Standard Workdays:

  • Employees are expected to work from Monday to Saturday each week.

Off Day:

  • Sunday is designated as the regular off day for all employees.

4.1.2 Public Holidays

Observation of Public Holidays:

  • All recognized public holidays are considered off days. Employees will not be required to work on these days unless approved by management.

Approval for Work on Public Holidays:

  • Exceptional Circumstances: In cases where business needs necessitate work on a public holiday, management may approve such requests on a case-by-case basis.
  • Compensatory Leave: Employees who work on a public holiday may be eligible for compensatory leave or additional incentives as determined by management.

4.2 Work Time and Attendance

4.2.1 Start Time (Work from Home)

Official Start Time:

  • All employees must commence their workday by 10:00 AM.

4.2.2 Morning Meetings

Mandatory Attendance:

  • Attendance at the daily morning meeting is mandatory for all employees.

Reporting Delays:

  • Notification Requirement: If an employee is unable to start work by 10:00 AM, they must inform their immediate superior before the start time, providing a valid reason for the delay.
  • Acceptable Reasons: Valid reasons may include medical emergencies, transportation issues, or other unforeseen circumstances.
  • Unapproved Absences: Failure to notify in advance may result in disciplinary action, including deductions in pay as outlined below.

4.2.3 Work Hours

Flexible Work Hours:

  • Elite Resumes does not enforce a fixed number of working hours per day. Instead, employees are expected to complete their daily assigned tasks within the workday.

Task Completion:

  • Responsibility: Employees are responsible for managing their time effectively to ensure that all assigned tasks are completed by the end of the workday.
  • Consequences of Non-Completion:
    • Partial Completion: Failure to deliver assigned tasks may result in a deduction of half a day's pay.
    • Repeated Non-Compliance: Continuous inability to meet task deadlines may lead to further disciplinary actions, including potential termination.

4.2.4 Monitoring and Availability

Communication Platforms:

  • Employees must remain available on WhatsApp during designated work hours for communication, collaboration, and task updates.

Availability Requirements:

  • Maximum Unavailability: Employees should not be unavailable for more than 30 minutes during work hours.

Notification Protocol:

  • Advance Notice: If an employee anticipates being unavailable, they must notify their higher official in advance, providing a valid reason.
  • Emergency Situations: In unforeseen emergencies, employees should inform their higher official as soon as possible.

Consequences of Unavailability:

  • Exceeded Unavailability: Being unavailable for more than 30 minutes without prior notification may result in a deduction of half a day's pay or a full day's pay loss, depending on the severity and frequency of the unavailability.

4.3 Payable Leaves

Elite Resumes offers various types of leaves to support the well-being and personal needs of its employees. The following outlines the payable leave policies:

4.3.1 Casual Leave

Entitlement:

  • Employees are entitled to one (1) casual leave with pay per month.

Usage:

  • Purpose: Casual leaves are intended for personal matters, emergencies, or unforeseen circumstances that require an employee's immediate attention.
  • Application Process: Employees must apply for casual leave in advance through the designated leave management system or inform their immediate supervisor as per company procedures.

4.3.2 Medical Leave

Entitlement:

  • Without Medical Certificate: Employees are entitled to one (1) medical leave per month without the need to submit a medical certificate.
  • With Medical Certificate: For more than one (1) medical leave within a month, employees must provide a valid medical certificate issued by a recognized medical practitioner.

Payment for Additional Medical Leaves:

  • Management Approval: Payment for medical leaves exceeding the monthly entitlement is subject to management's approval based on the circumstances and the validity of the medical certificate.
  • Documentation: Employees must submit all required documentation promptly to facilitate approval.

Usage:

  • Purpose: Medical leaves are intended for the recovery from illness, medical appointments, or any health-related issues requiring time off.

4.3.3 Leave Application Procedures

Advance Notice:

  • Employees are encouraged to apply for leaves in advance whenever possible to ensure adequate staffing and workflow continuity.

Emergency Leaves:

  • In cases of emergencies where advance notice is not feasible, employees must inform their immediate supervisor or higher official as soon as possible.

Approval Process:

  • Submission: All leave requests must be submitted through the designated leave management system or as per company procedures.
  • Review and Approval: Supervisors and management will review leave requests based on operational requirements and provide timely approvals or rejections.
  • Documentation: Employees must provide necessary documentation (e.g., medical certificates) when required to support their leave requests.

4.3.4 Consequences of Misuse of Leave Policies

  • Unauthorized Leaves: Taking leaves without proper authorization or falsifying leave reasons may result in disciplinary action, including deductions in pay or termination of employment.
  • Exceeding Leave Entitlements: Repeatedly exceeding leave entitlements without valid reasons or management approval may lead to further disciplinary measures.
